the coconut husk offers several benefits to the environment. You can use it to enrich your potting soil make a high-quality building board material ,and use it as an effective ground covering around small plants in your garden. You may also burn some husks to drive away mosquitoes. The smoke from a burning husk repels these insects. Although this is not an environmentally-friendly thing to do. In some cultures, particularly in the Philippines and Jamaica, they cut the dried and ripe coconut drupe crosswise and use it to buff floors. In the Philippines, they call it bunot (Tagalog) or lampaso (Visayan). In Jamaica, they call it coconut brush. Shell The most important of all is its utility as charcoal. It has high-class yield and produces better than coconut wood charcoal. Coconut shell charcoal is widely used both as a domestic and industrial fuel. As fuel for cooking, coconut shell charcoal burns hot and slow, leaving very little ash. It is hotter at 800℉ compared to natural lump that tends to run at tops of 735℉, and to briquettes at only 715℉. Coconut shell charcoal is also used to produce activated carbon, and as a major component in the manufacture of industrial products.
